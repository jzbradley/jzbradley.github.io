Benching
The practice of placing a potential relationship on standby, in anticipation of a more promising or interesting prospect, without entirely severing ties with the initial individual.
Caking
An enhanced form of flirting where an individual intensifies their compliments, displays increased interest, and exhibits heightened affection towards another.
Cloaking
An intensified form of ghosting where an individual not only ceases all communication but also takes measures to eliminate any online presence or connectivity with the other party, such as unfollowing or blocking them.
Cookie-Jarring
The act of treating an individual as a secondary or backup option in romantic pursuits while still maintaining a semblance of a relationship with them, often alongside other primary interests.
Daterview (date+interview)
A social interaction that, while intended to be a date, assumes the dynamics of an interview, emphasizing evaluation over casual or romantic engagement.
Love Bombing
An intense initiation of a relationship characterized by heightened affection, chemistry, and initiative from one partner, which abruptly ends without clear causation, differentiating itself from ghosting by the intensity and brevity of the affectionate phase.
Orbiting
The act of maintaining sporadic contact or presence in an ex-partner's life without re-engaging in a committed relationship. It signifies a latent interest without direct communication.
Pocketing
The act of maintaining a relationship with an individual without integrating them into one's broader social or family circle, typically due to uncertainty or hesitancy about the relationship's future.
Slow Fade
A gradual decrease in communication frequency and intensity, often resembling the effects of ghosting. Unlike direct break-ups, a slow fade sees partners distancing over time without a formal end to the relationship.
Zombie-ing
The recurrence of ghosting by an individual towards the same person. It involves a cycle where an individual who previously ceased all communication (ghosted) re-initiates contact, only to ghost again.
